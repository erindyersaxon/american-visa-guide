At the medical — what happens

Process steps, not things to bring. Know what to expect so nothing surprises you.

1 Check-in and digital photo
2 Document review
3 Chest X-ray (age 15+)
4 Blood test (age 18+)
5 Urine test (age 18+)
6 Physical examination
7 Vaccine review and administration Missing vaccinations required for US entry will be administered. Bring complete records to reduce the number of jabs needed.
! Before leaving — collect your pink checklist slip Required for the embassy document check the same day. Do not leave without it.

Same day — Embassy document check

Go directly to the embassy after the medical. Do not go home first.

The document check window closes at 1:00 PM. There is no other opportunity — missing it means rescheduling the whole day.

US Embassy London — 33 Nine Elms Lane, London SW11 7US

Window: 11:30 AM–1:00 PM · Queue opens approximately 11:45 AM

Enter at: Pass Back window on the right side of the building — do NOT join the main non-immigrant visa queue.

Bring to the embassy
At the embassy — what happens
1 Airport-style security — no large bags or laptops
2 Document check at window
3 Fingerprints
! Receive sealed "Do Not Tamper" envelope Keep sealed. Bring to interview day — do not open.

High-risk failure points

The most common reasons medical day goes wrong.

Missing GP summary — must be dated within 2–3 weeks of your appointment
Missing vaccination records — leads to unnecessary jabs or delays
Forgetting pink slip before leaving the IOM clinic — you cannot return for it
Not going to the embassy same day as the medical
Arriving after 1:00 PM and missing the document check window entirely
