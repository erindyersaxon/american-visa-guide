What officers typically ask

Most common questions (almost always asked):

  • How did you meet your spouse?
  • Where does your spouse currently live?
  • What does your spouse do for work?
  • What do you do for work?
  • Where will you live in the US?
  • Has either of you been married before?

Sometimes asked: Joint sponsor info, when you met in person, how long together, medical insurance plans, prior travel to US, children (if applicable).

Key insight: Officers are confirming what they already know from your application — not testing you. Be honest and conversational.

Timing reality

Total time in embassy: 1–3 hours

  • Check-in: 10–15 minutes (if you skip the main queue)
  • Wait for document handover: 30–90 minutes
  • Document handover: 2–5 minutes
  • Wait for interview: 30–120 minutes (longest part)
  • Interview itself: 2–5 minutes

Pro tip: Bring a book or download entertainment. No phone signal inside the embassy. Bathrooms available but limited.

What community says

  • Skip the queue: Go straight to the desk at the front. Tell staff "I have an immigrant visa interview."
  • Arrive 15–30 min early: Even though you'll wait, you skip the queue faster.
  • The most important documents are your sealed folder + passport: Everything else you bring is unlikely to be checked, but good to have as insurance.
  • Remember: No surprise documents that haven't already been uploaded to CEAC.
  • Wear business casual: Described as putting your best self forward.
  • Be friendly and conversational: Officers described as nice and relaxed. Some even joke.
  • Don't stress: By interview stage, you've been through the hardest parts!
  • Bring original beneficiary civil documents: e.g., birth certificate, marriage certificate, police certificates, military records, court records, etc.

Approval rates & next steps

~95% approved on the spot | ~3–4% 221(g) | ~1% refused

If approved: Told immediately. Given domestic violence pamphlet. Passport returned in 3–7 days (usually ~5 days). Visa issued same day or next day.

4. If approved

Passport is retained for visa printing. You'll receive an email from the embassy with collection instructions.

Typical passport return: Mail Boxes Etc (MBE) — 2–3 days for pickup, 5–7 days for mail delivery.

See data tracker for current community averages on passport return times. Next: Life in the USA

5. If not approved

221(g) (Administrative Processing): Case under review. You'll receive an email with next steps. Most resolve within weeks to months.

Visa Pause: If you hold dual citizenship and one country is on a pause list, wait for DOS update.

Denial: Rare. Officer explains reason. Limited recourse; consult attorney.
