Night before — pack these

Backup documents (bring, rarely requested)

ALL financial documents must already be uploaded to CEAC

The officer will not accept new documents at the window on interview day. Upload everything before you arrive.

Morning of interview — arriving

Inside — two steps

Total time inside: ~90–120 minutes. The wait is long; the interview is short.

Most of the time is waiting. The interview itself is typically 2–5 minutes.

Step 1 — Document window
→ Hand over passport and sealed document envelope
→ Confirm courier details for passport return
→ Fingerprints (if requested)
→ Sit back down and wait to be called to the interview window
Step 2 — Interview window
→ Take the oath
→ Answer questions — typically 2–5 minutes
→ Receive decision — usually immediate and verbal

Typical questions by visa category

These are the most commonly reported questions. Your interview may differ. Answers should match what is on your DS-260 and CEAC documents.

CR1 / IR1 — Spouse of US citizen

How and when did you meet?

Where does your spouse live? What does your spouse do for work?

What do you do for work?

Prior marriages?

Have you lived elsewhere / been arrested?

Who is your joint sponsor? (if applicable)

IR2 — Child of US citizen

Where does your parent live? What does your parent do for work?

When did you last see your parent?

Who do you currently live with?

Do you have siblings in the US?

IR5 — Parent of US citizen

How did your child become a US citizen?

Where does your child live? What does your child do for work?

Who is the joint sponsor? (if applicable)

Have you lived in the US before?

General — all categories

Have you ever been arrested?

Have you lived in other countries?

Have you been married before?

What are your plans in the US?

High-risk failure points

Joining the non-immigrant visa queue instead of going to the front
Forgetting the sealed envelope — this is the most critical item
Bringing a laptop or large bag — these cannot enter the building
Financial documents not already uploaded to CEAC before interview day
Answers inconsistent with your DS-260 or CEAC documents
Passport sticker missing correct name or case number — check at security

Non-UK nationals

Be aware of any potential visa pause or travel restriction issues for your nationality. Know your situation before walking in — this affects a small number of applicants but the impact is significant.

Bottom line

By this point USCIS, NVC, and medical have all cleared your case — this is a final consistency check

Passport + sealed envelope are the only essentials. Everything else is backup.

Skip the main queue. State "immigrant visa interview." Follow staff instructions.

Short interview, long wait — approval is typically immediate if documents were correct
