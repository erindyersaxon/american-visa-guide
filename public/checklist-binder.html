Stage 4 — Pre-Appointment · Reference

Visa Binder Layout

Document organisation for medical, document check, and interview

Checkboxes can be ticked in your browser. Your progress is not saved when you close or refresh the tab.

You need two trips to London — one for the medical and document check (same day), one for the interview. A well-organised binder means the document check window runs smoothly and you aren't scrambling on interview day. Staples (binder) and Amazon (coloured dividers) is a popular combination used by community members.

Front section — Immediate access

Front section — immediately accessible

These go at the very front. You will need these at security and at every window. Add the pink slip and sealed envelope on medical day.

Section 1

Section 1 — Medical documents

Used at: Medical appointment + document check

Section 2

Section 2 — Civil documents (originals + copies)

Used at: Document check (originals required) + interview (as backup)

Section 3

Section 3 — Financial documents (copies only)

Used at: Interview — backup copies only. Originals should not need to be presented.

All financial documents must be uploaded to CEAC before interview day

The officer will not accept new documents at the window. What is in your binder is backup — CEAC is what counts.

Section 4

Section 4 — Identity & travel documents

Used at: Interview — backup if requested

Section 5

Section 5 — Supporting / case-specific

As needed for your specific situation. Most applicants will not need this section.

Section 6

Section 6 — Optional / rarely requested

Backup only. Rarely requested at interview — do not rely on these for your primary submission.
