Overview

This is a guide to the consular visa process for family-based immigration through the US Embassy in London. It covers the steps from filing the I-130 petition through to arrival in the United States.

This process rewards overpreparation. When in doubt, follow the strictest interpretation of official guidance. Supply more evidence than asked. Document more than required. Leave nothing to chance.

This guide is written by and for community members who have been through it. It is not legal advice. Always consult with an immigration attorney for your specific situation.

Visa categories

Immediate Relative (IR) — No annual cap

Immediate relative visas have no annual cap. They include:

  • CR1/IR1 (Spouse): CR1 if married less than 2 years at US entry (conditional, 2-year validity). IR1 if married 2+ years (10-year validity). Marriage date matters. Keep original marriage certificate — CBP will ask for it at US entry.
  • IR2 (Unmarried child under 21): Child of US citizen petitioner
  • IR5 (Parent): Petitioner must be 21 or older

Family Preference (F) — Subject to annual cap

Family preference categories are subject to annual visa caps:

  • F1: Unmarried adult child (21+) of US citizen
  • F2A: Spouse/unmarried children of permanent resident
  • F2B: Unmarried adult child (21+) of permanent resident
  • F3: Married child of US citizen
  • F4: Sibling of US citizen (petitioner age 21+)

CRBA — Consular Report of Birth Abroad

If your child was born overseas to a US citizen parent, you can establish citizenship via CRBA. The US citizen parent must have lived in the US for 5+ years before the child's birth, including 2+ years after age 14.

Physical presence evidence—what works: Official school transcripts, W-2 forms with employer HR letter, passports showing entries/exits, military records, bank statements with US location markers, US medical records (with dates), CBP entry/exit records via FOIA.

Process: Register via MyTravelGov (eCRBA), pay $100, attend interview with child and US citizen parent, receive CRBA certificate and passport (~4 weeks).

Transgender and non-binary applicants

Current USCIS policy (April 2025) recognizes only male/female markers from original birth certificate. If your sex markers are inconsistent across documents, expect delays at USCIS, NVC, and medical stages. For legal assistance, contact Immigration Equality. Reference: USCIS Policy Manual Vol. 1, Part E, Chapter 5.

Filing the I-130

The I-130 petition establishes your relationship to the US petitioner and starts the visa process. It's submitted to USCIS and takes 9–18+ months to approve.

Petitioner requirements

  • US citizen or permanent resident
  • 18+ years old
  • Proof of identity and US immigration status
  • Income above poverty line for I-864 (affidavit of support)

Evidence checklist

  • Marriage certificate (if spouse petition)
  • Birth certificates
  • Divorce decrees (if previously married)
  • Photos of relationship
  • Communications (emails, messages, letters)
  • Travel records (passport stamps, flight records)

After submission

After USCIS approves the I-130 (NOA2), your case transfers to the National Visa Center (NVC).

National Visa Center (NVC)

Once USCIS approves the I-130, your case goes to the National Visa Center. NVC processes the case through 10 steps:

NVC sends a welcome letter 1–21 days after I-130 approval with your case number and invoice ID.

Log in to CEAC. Two fees: Visa processing $325 + Affidavit of Support $120.

Affidavit of Support (I-864)

  • Must be wet-signed (not digital)
  • Income must be 125% of federal poverty line
  • Attach IRS tax transcripts, W-2s, employment letters

DS-260 (Visa application)

  • Complete in CEAC
  • Form times out after ~10 minutes of inactivity
  • Prepare all answers in advance

Civil documents

  • Birth certificate
  • Marriage certificate
  • Police certificates (for all 16+)

Once all documents are submitted and reviewed, NVC marks your case as "Documentarily Qualified" (DQ). Your case then moves to the London Embassy queue.

Police certificates

Required for all applicants 16+ who hold other citizenship, live abroad 6+ months, or lived elsewhere 12+ months at age 16+.

For the UK: ACRO — Standard £68 / Premium £121. Validity: 12 months for embassy purposes.

12-month inactivity rule

INA §203(g): Your case will be terminated if you don't log into CEAC or contact NVC for 12 consecutive months. Log in every 6–11 months to keep your case active.

PCC warning

If you use a police certificate placeholder at NVC, you MUST upload the real certificate before your interview. If you don't, you risk administrative processing after your interview, which delays your visa.

Public charge

The "public charge" assessment is not a wealth test. It's a demonstration of your planning intent. Submitting a public charge statement is strongly advised.

Address: where you will live in the US, your employment plans, your health insurance plans. A specific plan significantly reduces risk.

Submit to LNDIVSubmissions@state.gov or upload to CEAC before interview.

Medical examination

CRITICAL: Your medical examination and document check happen on Trip 1. Your interview is on Trip 2. These are two separate visits to London on different days. They cannot be combined.

Medical provider

VisaMedicals — the only approved clinic for US visa medicals in the UK. Location: Bentinck Mansions, London

Booking

Medical appointment must be 10 working days before your interview. Slots fill quickly and open monthly. Phone: 020 7486 7822 | Email: bookings@visamedicals.co.uk

Fees (as of December 2025)

  • Adults 45+: £400
  • Adults 25–44: £430
  • Adults 18–24: £450
  • Children 15–17: £365
  • Children 14 and under: £250

What happens at the exam

Digital photo, document review, chest X-ray (15+), blood test (18+), urine test (18+), physical exam, vaccine review.

Vaccinations

MMR, Tetanus/Diphtheria, Hepatitis A & B (series can be completed in US), Varicella (or history), Polio (within 12 months), Flu (Oct–Mar). COVID-19 no longer required.

After medical

You'll receive a pink checklist slip. Go straight to the embassy for document check at the window on the right side of the building.

Document check window

11:45 AM to 1:00 PM. Present your pink slip, sealed packet from NVC. They will check that everything is in order.

Transgender applicants

Sex is determined by original birth certificate marker (male/female only). If your sex markers are inconsistent across documents, expect delays at the medical stage.

Interview

Your interview is at the US Embassy, 33 Nine Elms Lane, London SW11 7US.

What to bring

  • Passport
  • Sealed document packet (from document check)
  • Courier registration confirmation
  • Originals of everything else (though likely not reviewed)

Interview process

  1. Skip the main visa queue. Tell staff: "I am here for an immigrant visa interview."
  2. Document window: submit passport and sealed packet, confirm courier, fingerprints if requested.
  3. Interview window: oath, brief questions (typically 2–5 minutes), decision.

People permitted in the interview

  • NOT permitted: Petitioner, spouse, attorney
  • Permitted: Interpreter (if needed), one additional person for special needs only

Interview duration

Typical interview lasts under 5 minutes. Total time at embassy (including document window, waiting): 30 minutes to 2+ hours depending on queue.

After your interview

If approved

Your passport is retained for visa printing. You'll receive an email from the embassy with instructions on how to collect it.

Passport return methods:

  • 5–7 days after interview (up to 2 weeks)
  • Email notification from noreply@mbeglobal.com

If not approved: 221(g), visa pause, or denial

221(g) — Administrative Processing

Case is under review. You'll receive an email with instructions. Usually resolved within weeks to months. Most common reason: financial sponsorship review.

Visa pause

If you hold citizenship in a country on a visa pause list, your visa cannot be issued while the pause is in effect. If you have dual citizenship (e.g., UK + another country), attempt to use your other passport if possible.

Denial

Officer explains the reason. Recourse is limited but may include an I-601 waiver if you're eligible. Consult an immigration attorney.
