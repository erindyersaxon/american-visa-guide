The full pipeline — where things stand

Three stages connect your I-130 filing to a visa. They move independently — a stall at one stage does not affect the others, but builds a backlog that will be felt later.

⚠️
Stage 1 — I-130 at USCIS
Significantly slowed

The front of the line — the priority date of cases USCIS is actively working on — has not moved since 16 March 2026. As of mid-April 2026, USCIS was only processing cases with priority dates before 25 February 2025. Cases filed more recently are waiting in a growing queue.

Processing volumes have dropped to less than 50% of pre-slowdown levels. The cause is not confirmed — USCIS has not issued public guidance. Community reports suggest possible reassignment from service centres to field offices.

Data: Track My Visa — April 2026 analysis ↗ Live I-130 tracker ↗
✅
Stage 2 — National Visa Center (NVC)
Processing normally

After working through the steps at NVC, the time from submitting your forms and evidence to being Documentarily Qualified (DQ) is typically 10 business days (~14 calendar days).

If you receive an RFE (Request for Further Evidence) at NVC, you will need to respond and resubmit — the 14-day clock then restarts from that resubmission. Some applicants also hold their case at NVC deliberately. Guide note →

✅
Stage 3 — London Embassy queue
IL drops continuing

Interview Letters are continuing to be issued. The DQ → IL wait has lengthened in recent months but drops are still occurring on a regular cadence. The separate visa pause affects a small number of individual cases — it is not a general halt on London processing.

☕ What this means for you

If you were documentarily qualified (DQ) recently, you're likely looking at an Interview Letter around 8 weeks after your DQ date — though the 6-month trend is running longer (see trend table below).

Once your Interview Letter arrives, your interview is typically about 9 weeks further out. DQ to sitting in the embassy: roughly 4 months.

After a successful interview, most members have their passport back within a week — pick-up averages 4 days, mail averages 12 days. The vast majority of interviews result in same-day approval.

The latest IL drop was 17 April 2026. The next window is estimated around 10 May – 20 May 2026.

What this means for you: If your case is already at NVC or beyond, the I-130 slowdown does not affect you directly. However, a prolonged stall builds a backlog of cases that will enter NVC and the London embassy queue once processing resumes — those already in the pipeline will face increased competition for interview slots. If you are still waiting on I-130 approval, plan for significantly longer than the historical 12–18 month average.
